Fake Uniswap Google Ads Phishing Scam Investigation Report

Investigation Date: May 26, 2026
Event Type: Google Ads Phishing Scam
Loss Amount: $400,000+
Attacker Wallets: - 0x37925684BA178821b4436E06e67f5dBD6cfA49Bb - 0x2fC25F46cCC49D226eF92E9A7665f3d2821F3c5E2

📋 Event Overview

On May 25, 2026, on-chain analyst b_block discovered attackers purchased Google sponsored ads to impersonate the official Uniswap website, inducing users to connect wallets and sign malicious transactions, thereby stealing user assets.

As of the report, the two attacker wallets hold approximately 146 ETH (valued ~$306,000 at the time), with total losses exceeding $400,000.

🔍 Attack Method Analysis

Attack Flow

  1. Ad Placement: Attackers purchased "Uniswap" keyword sponsored ads on Google search platform
  2. Phishing Website: Users clicking ads were directed to a meticulously designed phishing website, nearly identical to the official site
  3. Malicious Authorization: When users connected wallets and signed transactions, they actually authorized malicious contract access
  4. Fund Transfer: Drainer contract automatically transferred user assets to attacker-controlled wallets

Attack Tools

Security researchers identified the phishing website used the AngelFerno drainer tool, a "Phishing-as-a-Service" malware.

Attackers also employed the following techniques to evade detection: - Punycode URL: Using Cyrillic letters to make phishing domain visually indistinguishable from legitimate domain - Hidden iframe: Loads malicious code invisible to Google's automated review system - Traffic Redirection: All user network traffic secretly routed to attacker-controlled servers

Attacker Infrastructure

  • Phishing websites used Google trusted services (sites.google.com, docs.google.com) to bypass detection
  • Advanced infrastructure including Cloudflare Workers, Arweave hosting payloads, and proxy layers
  • Can intercept Ethereum RPC requests and monitor user activity in real-time

⚠️ Systemic Risk Analysis

Google Ads Platform Responsibility

According to the Security Alliance (SEAL) report: - During March 2026, Google Ads phishing attacks stole approximately $1.27 million - SEAL blocks over 356 malicious Google ad links weekly - This attack pattern has persisted for over one year with no signs of slowing

Notable Victims

Uniswap founder Hayden Adams has publicly criticized Google multiple times for failing to effectively combat counterfeit ads:

"These scams are terrible. We've been fighting them for years. Fake apps impersonating Uniswap keep appearing, even though we've been applying to the Apple App Store and waiting months for approval."

FBI Data

According to FBI's "2025 Internet Crime Report": - Cryptocurrency-related complaints: 181,565 - Total losses: $11.36 billion (22% year-over-year increase) - Average loss per victim: $62,604

🛡️ Community Protection Recommendations

  1. Bookmark URLs: Manually bookmark DeFi platform URLs instead of relying on search
  2. Manual URL Entry: Enter official domains directly in browser
  3. Verify Through Trusted Aggregators: Verify protocol information through trusted aggregators like DeFiLlama
  4. Regular Authorization Revocation: Use revoke.cash to regularly clean up unnecessary token authorizations
  5. Hardware Wallet: Use hardware wallets and carefully review each transaction
  6. Ad Blocking: Consider using ad-blocking plugins and anti-phishing browser extensions

📝 Unique Analysis Perspective

This case reveals the contradiction between centralized platforms and decentralized finance:

  • DeFi protocols themselves are secure: Uniswap smart contracts have never been hacked
  • The problem is at the entry point: Google search results have become accomplices for attackers
  • Irreversibility: Blockchain transactions cannot be reversed; once malicious transactions are signed, funds cannot be recovered

This differs from traditional cybersecurity—users cannot "call customer service" or "request a refund"; they can only rely on prevention rather than remediation.
