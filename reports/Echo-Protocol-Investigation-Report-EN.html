Echo Protocol eBTC Admin Key Attack Investigation Report

Date: May 27, 2026
Event: Echo Protocol eBTC Admin Key Attack
Attack Time: May 18, 2026 ~17:55 ET
Reporter: Independent On-Chain Investigator

Executive Summary

BTCFi protocol Echo Protocol's eBTC deployment on Monad suffered an admin key attack. The attacker obtained DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE and self-granted MINTER_ROLE, minting 1,000 unsecured eBTC (face value $76.7M), cashing out ~$816K in real assets through Curvance lending protocol before laundering through Tornado Cash. Due to insufficient Monad DeFi ecosystem liquidity, 955 eBTC could not be liquidated and were ultimately burned by the Echo team.

Key Lesson: A $254M+ TVL protocol, with management permissions attached to a single EOA private key—one key is the entire defense line.

Attack Flow Analysis

Step 1: Obtain Admin Privileges

Attacker obtained control of DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE for the eBTC contract. This permission was attached to a single EOA address (regular wallet, one private key only), with no multisig protection, no timelock, and no rate limiting.

Step 2: Self-Grant Minter Role

grantRole(MINTER_ROLE, attacker_wallet)

Used admin privileges to grant themselves a minting role.

Step 3: Mint Fake eBTC

mint(attacker_wallet, 1000e8)

1,000 eBTC appeared from thin air. Face value $76.7M, real BTC backing: 0.

Step 4: Cover Tracks

Attacker revoked their own admin privileges, making on-chain traces less obvious. This was premeditated—knowing investigators would first scan role grant records.

Step 5: Cash Out Through Curvance

  • Deposited 45 fake eBTC (face value $3.45M) into Curvance as collateral
  • Curvance had no verification to distinguish real from fake eBTC—from the contract's perspective, eBTC is just eBTC
  • Borrowed 11.29 WBTC (~$867,700)

Step 6: Cross-Chain Laundering

  • Bridged WBTC to Ethereum mainnet
  • Swapped for ETH
  • ~384 ETH ($821,700) deposited to Tornado Cash

Step 7: Remaining Fake Coins Stranded

955 eBTC left in attacker's Monad wallet, unable to cash out further due to liquidity depletion. Echo team subsequently burned these tokens.

Dual Failure Analysis

Failure 1: Echo Protocol — Single Private Key Managing $254M+ Protocol

  • DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE attached to an EOA
  • No multisig, no timelock, no mint cap, no rate limit
  • Entire Monad deployment security is equivalent to one private key's security

Failure 2: Curvance — No Collateral Source Verification

  • Accepted newly minted eBTC as collateral without verifying BTC backing
  • Lending protocols should implement post-mint cooldown or whitelist mechanisms
  • Isolated market design limited contagion but didn't prevent single-asset exploitation

Defense Recommendations

For Protocol Teams

  1. Multisig Management: Minimum 2-of-3, recommend 3-of-5 + hardware wallets
  2. Timelock: Ownership changes require 24-48 hour delay
  3. Mint Caps: Single/daily minting limits
  4. Rate Limiting: Large mints trigger alerts and delays
  5. Role Separation: Admin/minter/pauser use different controllers

For Lending Protocols

  1. Collateral Source Verification: Newly minted tokens need cooldown before collateral eligibility
  2. Mint Monitoring: Real-time monitoring for abnormal supply growth
  3. Isolated Markets: Curvance's isolated market design limited contagion—well done on this front
